Why GIWA — Technical Rationale

Why KorriPay Belongs on GIWA

memory

Osaka EVM Precompiles

GIWA's Osaka EVM hardfork exposes native MODEXP and P256VERIFY precompiles at near-zero gas cost. KorriPay leverages these to run compliance passport checks and settlement proof generation on-chain without prohibitive gas overhead.

layers

GIWA L2 Rollup Architecture

GIWA's optimistic rollup sequencer finality enables sub-10-second settlement confirmation. Rollup batching reduces settlement gas costs by ~87% vs mainnet. KorriPay anchors every settlement proof to a GIWA L2 block for immutable, verifiable finality.

currency_exchange

GIWA's KRW Stablecoin Ecosystem

GIWA is building a regulated KRW stablecoin ecosystem natively. KorriPay's KRWC Test Asset (backed by MockKRWStable contract) is the prototype integration point. When GIWA's production KRW stablecoin launches, KorriPay becomes the first institutional settlement infrastructure ready to process it.

hub

Not a Generic EVM Chain

Generic EVM chains have no institutional identity layer, no precompile settlement hooks, and no KRW liquidity strategy. GIWA is purpose-built for institutional clearing. KorriPay is purpose-built for institutional settlement. They belong together.

Settlement Pipeline

The Settlement Engine

KorriPay executes settlements through an automated three-step state transition flow.

1

Initialize & Lock

Funds are debited from the sender's available balance and moved to "Locked" status to guarantee settlement availability.

2

On-Chain Settlement

Smart contracts verify compliance keys and trigger a cryptographic settlement of assets in the settlement queue.

3

Disburse & Audit

Recipient balance is credited immediately, and immutable settlement proofs and PDF records are archived.

Ethereum Attestation Service

Attestation Layer (EAS)

KorriPay leverages EAS schemas to issue cryptographic proofs for merchants, businesses, and compliance events.

verified

Verifiable Credentials

Merchants and users maintain cryptographic credentials proving status without exposing raw personal identity datasets.

lock

Zero-Knowledge Verification

Proofs are checked cryptographically in the smart contract prior to executing transactions, preventing regulatory exposure.
